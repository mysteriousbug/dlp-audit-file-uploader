[THEME 1: EXPERIENCE OF WORKING WITH THE BANK]
For experience of working with the bank and GIAI.
First, improving audit scheduling to avoid overlapping bookings and building in recovery time between major audits. Currently, colleagues are transitioning from one intense audit directly into another with no buffer. This impacts both sustainability and work quality.

[THEME 2: WORK ENVIRONMENT]
For work environment.
First, reinforcing psychological safety by eliminating blame and defensive reactions. Creating an environment where colleagues feel safe admitting mistakes or asking for help prevents issues from being hidden until they escalate.
Second, increasing informal team interactions and cross-location connections. I should note that we currently send out team lunch meeting placeholders monthly, but participation has been very low. If we believe these interactions are valuable, we might need to approach them differently to encourage actual attendance.
Third, recognizing teamwork and collaboration, not only output quantity. This would signal that how work is accomplished matters, not just volume of output.
Fourth, building in downtime after heavy cycles. Scheduling recovery time after intensive audit periods would support sustainability and quality.
[THEME 3: FUTURE READINESS]
For future readiness, particularly around AI.
First, protecting dedicated learning time in the audit calendar. This would mean actually blocking time and respecting those blocks, rather than encouraging learning while making it practically impossible due to workload.
Second, building structured capability development pathways for AI, data, and domain skills. Our colleagues need a clear path from current capability to required capability, not just ad-hoc training offerings.
Third, focusing training on audit-relevant use cases and linking to development plans. Practical, directly applicable training would be more valuable than theoretical concepts. Colleagues need to understand specifically how AI will affect their audit work and what tools they'll be using.
[THEME 4: GROWTH AND PROMOTION]
For growth and promotion.
First, publishing transparent and standardized promotion and evaluation criteria. Written, public criteria would help our colleagues understand exactly what's required for progression and ensure consistency across the organization.
Second, scheduling formal career development discussions more frequently. Quarterly conversations, rather than only annual reviews, would make career development an ongoing dialogue rather than an annual event.
Third, introducing mentorship programs and targeted domain-expertise refresh programs. This would provide colleagues access to experienced peers for guidance and keep skills current in evolving areas.
[THEME 5: PEOPLE LEADER]
For People Leader engagement.
First, empowering PLs to manage workload and resource constraints. This would mean giving People Leaders actual authority to push back on unrealistic demands and redistribute work, not just asking them to support work-life balance without the tools to do so.
Second, creating trackers for feedback outcomes and actions taken. When colleagues raise issues, tracking what happens next creates accountability and demonstrates that feedback leads to action.
Third, requiring monthly one-on-ones focused on wellbeing and development. These would be substantive conversations about how colleagues are doing and where they want to grow, not just project status updates.
Fourth, fostering a culture of coaching and accountability rather than blame. When issues arise, the focus would be on learning rather than fault-finding. This approach develops capability rather than creating defensiveness.
Fifth, standardizing feedback quality across PLs. This would ensure all colleagues receive regular, specific, actionable feedback, not vague generalizations.
